ARJUNO
Mount Arjuno is a stratovolcano located in East Java, Indonesia. It stands at an elevation of 3,339 meters (10,922 feet) and is part of the Bromo Tengger Semeru National Park. Known for its symmetrical cone shape, Arjuno is an active volcano, though its last eruption was in the late 19th century.

The mountain is popular among trekkers and climbers for its scenic views and challenging ascent. It is often paired with nearby Mount Welirang, another active volcano, in climbing expeditions. The surrounding area is lush with forests, offering rich biodiversity and a cool, refreshing climate.

CIREMAI
Mount Ciremai, or Gunung Ciremai, is the highest mountain in West Java, Indonesia, standing at 3,078 meters (10,097 feet) above sea level. It is an active stratovolcano located within the Ciremai National Park, which is renowned for its diverse flora and fauna.

The surrounding area is also culturally significant, with many villages at its base and within its vicinity, offering visitors a mix of natural beauty and local culture.

GEDE
Mount Gede is an active stratovolcano located in West Java, Indonesia. It stands at 2,958 meters (9,702 feet) above sea level and is part of the Gede-Pangrango volcanic complex. Mount Gede is known for its lush surrounding environment, which includes the Gede Pangrango National Park.

The volcano has been active in the past, with the last eruption occurring in the 1950s. Hikers and nature enthusiasts frequent the mountain, as it offers well-maintained trails, scenic views, and diverse flora and fauna. The summit provides panoramic views of the surrounding area, including nearby Mount Pangrango. Mount Gede is also a popular destination for birdwatching, given its rich biodiversity.

KAWAH RATU
Mount Kawah Ratu is an active volcano located in the West Java province of Indonesia. It is part of the volcanic complex of the Gede-Pangrango mountain range and is situated within the Gede Pangrango National Park. The volcano's name, "Kawah Ratu," translates to "Queen's Crater," referring to its large crater, which is a key feature of the mountain.

The crater of Kawah Ratu contains sulfur deposits, and visitors can often see steam vents and hot springs, making it a popular attraction for those interested in volcanic landscapes. While it is an active volcano, it has relatively mild eruptions, with most of its activity consisting of frequent fumarolic (gas) emissions.

The surrounding area is rich in biodiversity and attracts hikers and nature enthusiasts. The peak of Kawah Ratu stands at an altitude of approximately 2,386 meters (7,828 feet). The mountain also holds cultural and spiritual significance for local communities.
