Arjuno Mountain

Arjuno Mountain (Gunung Arjuno) is a stratovolcano located in East Java, Indonesia. Standing at an elevation of approximately 3,339 meters (10,955 feet) above sea level, it is one of the highest mountains in Java and a popular destination for hikers and nature enthusiasts.
Key Changes and Features:
1. Arjuno is part of the Tengger massif and is closely associated with its neighboring mountain, Welirang. Together, they form the Arjuno-Welirang complex.
2. The name Arjuno is derived from Arjuna, a heroic figure from the Hindu epic Mahabharata.
3. Popular hiking route starting points for ascents include Lawang, Tretes, and Batu.
